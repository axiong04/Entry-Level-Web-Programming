Ben Frankin's Life

On a cold Boston night, Benjamin Franklin was born on January 7th, 1706. He was sent to school, but his parents could only afford two years of school. He left for philadephia and worked many odd jobs until he published Poor Richard's Almanack, a resounding success, making him a wealth man. After his successes, he was unofficially retired, making use of his time pursuing literature and the arts. However, he was called upon to become an ambassador in France, where he negotiated with the French to help in the Revolutionary war. He also helped drafting the Declaration of Independance. After a fuifilling life as a writer, musician, and founding father, he died at age 84 in his home.
